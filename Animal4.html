Marine Habitat

Marine habitats are habitats that support marine life.
Marine life depends in some way on the saltwater that is
in the sea. A habitat is an ecological or environmental
area inhabited by one or more living species. The marine
environment supports many kinds of these habitats.

Orca Whale


The orca or killer whale is a toothed whale belonging
to the oceanic dolphin family, of which it is the largest
member. It is recognizable by its black-and-white patterned
body.

Giant Clam


The giant clams are the members of the clam genus Tridacna
that are the largest living bivalve mollusks. There are actually
several species of "giant clams" in the genus Tridacna, which are
often misidentified for Tridacna gigas, the most commonly intended
species referred to as "the giant clam".

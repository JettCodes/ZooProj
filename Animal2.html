Desert Habitat

Deserts are the driest places on Earth—they get fewer than 10 inches (25
centimeters) of rain a year. Some deserts may get a lot of rain all at once.
Then it might not rain again for months—or even years!


Camel



A camel is an even-toed ungulate in the genus Camelus that bears
distinctive fatty deposits known as "humps" on its back. Camels
have long been domesticated and, as livestock, they provide food
and textiles.

Fennec Fox


The fennec fox is a small crepuscular fox native to the deserts of North Africa,
ranging from Western Sahara to the Sinai Peninsula. Its most distinctive feature
is its unusually large ears, which serve to dissipate heat and listen for underground
prey. The fennec is the smallest species of fox.
